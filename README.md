## `whoami`

DevSecOps at **Tetra Software**, running out of Da Nang, Vietnam.

My job is to keep the interesting parts interesting and the production parts boring. Pipelines that fail loudly at 3pm, not quietly at 3am.

### What I actually do

- **Ship it** — self-hosted Git, Actions runners, container registries, reproducible builds. If a deploy needs a human awake at midnight, that's a bug, not a process.
- **Zero trust, not zero access** — admin planes live on a private overlay, nothing sensitive listens on a public port, SSO in front of everything internal.
- **Harden and watch** — shrinking attack surface, reverse proxies that know the real client IP, an IPS that earns its keep, and reading the logs nobody else reads.
- **Virtualization** — hypervisors, VM fleets, and a permanent war against disk I/O.
